Burden of Proof in Civil vs. Criminal Court

In civil court cases, the burden of proof lies with the plaintiff, who must establish their claims based on a "preponderance of the evidence." This means that the plaintiff must show it is more likely than not that their assertions are true. For Florida shooting victims, this allows specific victims to pursue compensation from the responsible party by satisfying this lower standard of proof. In contrast, criminal cases require the prosecutor to meet a much higher threshold known as "beyond a reasonable doubt." This rigorous standard reflects the serious consequences that can arise in crime victim cases, such as incarceration for the accused.

The differences in the burden of proof highlight the complexities within civil vs. criminal cases for Florida shooting victims: key differences.
